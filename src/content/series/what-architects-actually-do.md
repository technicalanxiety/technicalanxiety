## Overview

Architecture isn't about drawing diagrams. It's about translation, credibility, and making technical decisions that survive contact with reality.

## What You'll Learn

### The Translation Layer
- Converting business needs into technical requirements
- Explaining technical constraints to non-technical stakeholders
- Building shared understanding across domains
- Navigating conflicting priorities

### Maintaining Credibility
- Staying relevant when practitioners know more than you
- Balancing breadth and depth
- Knowing when to defer to specialists
- Building trust through technical competence

### Making Decisions Stick
- Architecture decisions that teams actually follow
- Documentation that people read
- Governance without bureaucracy
- Measuring architectural success

## Who This Is For

- **Architects** figuring out what the job actually is
- **Technical Leaders** transitioning to architecture roles
- **Engineers** considering architecture paths
- **Managers** working with architects
